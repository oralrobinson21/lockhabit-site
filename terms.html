3. Savings “Lock” Accounts

LockHabit provides a behavioral “hard-lock” savings mechanism. You choose a term and optionally set up recurring contributions. Funds are reserved for you and not lent to others by LockHabit.

3.1 Lock Terms & APR Tiers

  • APR Tiers (illustrative, subject to change): 1–3 months: 1.5–2.0% APR; 4–6 months: 2.5–3.0% APR; 6–12+ months: 3.25–3.5% APR.
  • APR is prorated based on time in lock. Rates may change with notice; final APY and disclosures will be shown at account opening.
  • Auto-Upgrade: If you extend your lock to a higher tier, the new APR takes effect on the next cycle date (or within 14 days), and continues while the new term is active.

3.2 Early Withdrawal Policy

Locks are intended to be held to maturity. Non-emergency early withdrawals incur a fee to discourage impulsive breaks and protect program stability:

  • Standard early-break fee: 5–10% of the amount withdrawn (final schedule disclosed at account opening).
  • Relock Grace: If you relock the withdrawn amount within 48 hours, LockHabit may reduce the early-break fee by up to 50% (promotional/eligibility rules apply).

4. Self-Backed Loans

Borrowing is secured by your locked balance and designed to help you avoid early-break fees.

4.1 Standard Self-Loan

  • Maximum loan amount: up to 75–80% of current eligible balance.
  • APR: typically 6–8% (subject to change), credited back to your lock after full payoff.
  • Payment reporting: intended to be reported to major bureaus; on-time payments may help build credit; late/missed payments may harm credit.
  • Two (2) skip-a-payment options per 12-month period, which extend the loan term by one month each. Interest continues to accrue during skipped months.

4.2 Emergency Loan (EAP)

  • Eligibility: verified hardship (e.g., job loss, medical, disaster). Documentation may be required.
  • Amount: up to 90% of eligible balance. Intro APR: 0% for 3–6 months (term varies on qualification).
  • After the intro term, standard program APR may apply to any unpaid principal.

4.3 APR During Outstanding Loans

  • While a self-loan is outstanding, the APR on the remaining locked funds may be reduced to 1–1.5% until the loan is brought current or repaid.
  • When the loan is current for 2 consecutive cycles or fully repaid, the lock APR may return to the applicable tier.
