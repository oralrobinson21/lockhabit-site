Investor Summary

Discipline-first savings for working Americans. Users lock what they meant to save, balances compound weekly, and a self-backed emergency line (up to 80% of vested) prevents a single crisis from destroying the habit.

Highlights (2-minute scan)

  • Core APR tiers: 2.0% / 2.5% / 3.0% / 3.5% (weekly compounding)
  • Emergency line: 80% LTV; 0% for 4 months, then 6% APR; interest credited back after payoff
  • Funds with partner banks; deposits are never lent to other customers
  • Transparent, flat unlock fees; credit-builder reporting intended

Purpose & Impact

People don’t fail at math—they fail at behavior. The current system makes saving frictionless to start and frictionless to quit. LockHabit flips that: we add just enough productive friction to protect intent. Users lock funds on a schedule, watch progress compound, and when life happens, they get controlled access without predatory terms.

We serve workers who already know what they should do—our product helps them actually do it. The outcome is less stress, fewer debt spirals, and a repeatable savings habit that builds confidence month after month.

Problem

Traditional savings is liquid by design—great for banks, terrible for behavior. A single unexpected expense often empties accounts and resets progress. Credit steps in with interest that compounds the wrong way.

Solution

A voluntary hard-lock that rewards time, not spend. Weekly compounding reinforces momentum. A self-backed emergency line against the user’s vested balance preserves the habit instead of punishing the emergency.

Model (high-level)

  • Programmatic yield share with bank partners (float)
  • Flat unlock fee by term ($1–$3), clearly disclosed
  • Optional High-Risk Growth (7.8–20% variable APR; may lose value)
  • No lending of customer deposits to other customers

We intentionally omit specific unit economics and partner terms on this public page.
